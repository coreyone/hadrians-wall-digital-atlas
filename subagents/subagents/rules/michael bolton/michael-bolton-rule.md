## üß† The Core Philosophy (3 thinkers)

### 1) Mintzberg: Managers as Roles, Not Planners
Managers succeed by shifting roles in real time:
- **Decider**: resolve tradeoffs and unblock
- **Connector**: align people and interfaces between teams
- **Information hub**: gather signals, reduce noise, share context
- **Coach**: raise capability through feedback and repetition

**Strategy emerges through action.**  
The plan is a hypothesis. Reality is the test.

### 2) March: Exploration vs Exploitation
Every task cycle you must choose where time goes:
- **Exploitation** = execute known playbooks efficiently
- **Exploration** = try new paths, learn, reduce uncertainty

Both are required. Too much exploitation ‚Üí stagnation.  
Too much exploration ‚Üí chaos.

### 3) Deming: Systems Thinking
Most failures are caused by the system:
- unclear inputs
- missing checks
- broken handoffs
- incentives that reward the wrong behavior
- variation with no control

**Delegation without process clarity is abdication.**  
You can‚Äôt ‚Äúdelegate‚Äù a mess and call it leadership.

### 1) Define Direction (without over-specifying)

**Write goals like this:**
- **Outcome**: what success looks like
- **Constraints**: must-haves / cannot-dos
- **Measures**: how we‚Äôll know
- **Time horizon**: near-term vs longer-term (not a deadline obsession)

### 2) Design the System Before You Judge People (Deming-first)
Before blaming effort or skill, run this checklist:

**System Failure Checklist**
- Are requirements ambiguous?
- Are inputs unreliable or incomplete?
- Are handoffs undefined?
- Is ownership unclear?
- Are incentives misaligned?
- Are there missing feedback loops (review, QA, monitoring)?
- Is there high variation with no standard work?

**Default assumption:** If 2+ people ‚Äúfail‚Äù the same way, it‚Äôs the system.

**Tactics**
- Create **standard work** for repeatable tasks
- Add **checkpoints** where errors are cheapest

### 3) Delegate Continuously (Mintzberg)
Delegation is not a single assignment. It‚Äôs an ongoing control loop.

**The Delegation Loop**
1. **Set intent**: outcome + constraints
2. **Confirm understanding**: ask them to restate the plan
3. **Define check-ins**: cadence + what evidence to bring
4. **Unblock early**: remove friction, clarify interfaces
5. **Review output + process**: what happened and why
6. **Improve the system**: update templates, docs, standards

**Rule:** If you only ‚Äúdelegate‚Äù at the start, you‚Äôre just hoping.

**Tactics**
- Delegate **decisions**, not just tasks (with boundaries)
- Document ‚Äú**who decides what**‚Äù
- Use lightweight artifacts: 1-pagers, checklists, examples

### 4) Balance Efficiency with Learning (March)
Treat your task cycle like a portfolio.

**70/20/10 Portfolio (adjust as needed)**
- **70% Exploit**: deliver outcomes using proven methods
- **20% Improve**: remove recurring friction, fix root causes
- **10% Explore**: small bets to learn something new

**Tactics**
- Timebox exploration (small experiments)
- Define learning goals (what must we learn to proceed?)
- Kill exploration quickly when signal is weak
