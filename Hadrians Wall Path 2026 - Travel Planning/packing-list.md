## On-Trail: Technical Clothing (The 3-Layer System)
*Refreshed for April: Cool, damp, and changeable.*
- [ ] **Base Layers (x2):** Merino wool or synthetic (e.g., Smartwool, Patagonia Capilene). *No cotton!*
- [ ] **Mid Layer - Fleece:** A breathable insulating layer (e.g., Polartec fleece).
- [ ] **Mid Layer - "Puffy":** Lightweight synthetic or down jacket for stops and chilly sections.
- [ ] **Outer Shell:** High-quality waterproof jacket (Gore-Tex or similar, 15k+ HH rating).
- [ ] **Waterproof Trousers:** Packable over-trousers for sudden rain.
- [ ] **Hiking Socks (x3):** High-quality Merino (e.g., Darn Tough or Bridgedale).
- [ ] **Liner Socks (Optional):** If prone to blisters.
- [ ] **Accessories:** Waterproof gloves, warm beanie, and a neck buff.

## On-Trail: Gear & Navigation
- [ ] **Boots:** Waterproof hiking boots (mid-cut for mud and ankle support). Well broken-in!
- [ ] **Daypack (20-30L):** Must have a waterproof rain cover.
- [ ] **Water Storage:** 2L total (Bladder or bottles).
- [ ] **Navigation:** Physical trail map + guidebook (signal is spotty).
- [ ] **Power:** 10,000mAh+ Power bank + charging cables.
- [ ] **Dry Bags:** To keep electronics and spare clothes dry inside your pack.

## Off-Trail: The "Pub Uniform" (Lodging & Celebrations)
- [ ] **Evening Trousers:** Lightweight, wrinkle-resistant chinos or tech-pants.
- [ ] **Fresh Shirts (x2):** Stylish but packable.
- [ ] **Communal Footwear:** Lightweight sneakers or sandals (to air out your feet).
- [ ] **Sleepwear:** Lightweight.

## The "Foot First Aid" & Toiletries
- [ ] **Blister Prevention:** Leukotape or Zinc Oxide tape (apply to "hot spots" early).
- [ ] **Blister Treatment:** Compeed hydrocolloid patches.
- [ ] **Personal Toiletries:** Minimalist (B&Bs provide towels).
- [ ] **Sunscreen & Lip Balm:** Windburn is common in April.
- [ ] **Cash:** Small notes for honesty boxes and trail snacks.

---
**Note:** Assume a **20kg limit** for luggage transfer services. Carry only what you need for the day in your daypack.
