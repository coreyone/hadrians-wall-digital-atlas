## Why It Matters Here
Hadrian's Wall isn't just a line on a map - it's a living corridor of **pubs, farms, market towns, and Roman ruins**. The food culture mirrors that: practical, seasonal, and proud. You'll taste **Northumbrian and Cumbrian staples** shaped by long winters, fishing villages, and farm life.

## Must-Try Local Specialties (Checklist)
- [ ] **Stottie cake** - a dense, chewy bread roll; perfect for breakfast sandwiches.  
  Learn more: [Stottie cake (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stottie_cake).
- [ ] **Pease pudding** - savory split-pea spread, often with ham.  
  Learn more: [Pease pudding (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pease_pudding).
- [ ] **Cumberland sausage** - coiled, peppery, and protected as a regional specialty.  
  Learn more: [Cumberland sausage (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cumberland_sausage).
- [ ] **Craster kippers** - smoked herring from the Northumberland coast.  
  Learn more: [Craster kippers (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Craster#Kippers).
- [ ] **Singing hinny** - griddled Northumbrian pastry.  
  Learn more: [Singing hinny (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singing_hinny).
- [ ] **Pan haggerty** - sliced potato, onion, and cheese bake (Northumberland).  
  Learn more: [Pan haggerty (Northumberland Gazette)](https://www.northumberlandgazette.co.uk/whats-on/arts-and-entertainment/celebrate-northumberland-day-with-this-traditional-pan-haggerty-recipe-3763414).
- [ ] **Newcastle Brown Ale** - iconic local beer style.  
  Learn more: [Newcastle Brown Ale (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newcastle_Brown_Ale).
- [ ] **Sticky toffee pudding (Cartmel)** - Lake District legend and worth a detour if you swing south.  
  Learn more: [Sticky toffee pudding (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sticky_toffee_pudding).

## Eating Guidelines & Practical Tips
- **Pub food** is your friend. It's warm, filling, and usually served all afternoon.
- **"Last orders"** can be earlier in small villages; ask at check-in and plan dinner timing.
- **Dietary needs** are common and usually accommodated, but rural kitchens are smaller - call ahead when possible.

## Restaurant Pricing & Hours (What to Expect)
- **Lunch is the value meal**: set menus and smaller plates are common.
- **Dinner** in villages can be limited to a few places; book ahead for weekends.
- **Sunday roast** is popular and often sells out.

## Breakfast
- **Full English** or a **stottie breakfast sandwich** is ideal before long miles.  
  Learn more: [Full breakfast (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Full_breakfast).
- Many B&Bs include breakfast; confirm early/packed options on big walking days.

## Budget Eating
- **Market food** and bakeries stretch your budget without feeling cheap.
- **Meal deals** (sandwich + snack + drink) are common in UK convenience shops.

## A Few Local "Yes" Rules
- If a pub has **locals at the bar at 5 pm**, it's likely a good choice.
- If you see **"homemade pies"**, that's almost always a win.
- If you want a true local vibe, pick a place **one street off** the main square.
