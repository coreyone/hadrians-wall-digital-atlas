# Practicalities at the Destination
## Document Goals
- This document is everything you need to know to travel smoothly.
- It includes practical travel tips and money tips (ATM use, card acceptance, etc).
- It includes electronic adapter info and tipping customs.
- It includes common closures for tourist activities (museums, sights, shops, restaurants).
- It includes getting around tips (ride share availability, walking safety, etc).

This is everything you need to travel smoothly - money, power, weather, transport, and the small stuff that makes a big difference in April.

## Money & Payments
- Currency: **British Pound Sterling (GBP)**.  
  Learn more: [Pound sterling (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pound_sterling).
- Cards are widely accepted in towns; **carry some cash** for small villages and trail snacks.
- **Service charge** is common in sit-down restaurants in the UK; check the bill before tipping extra.  
  Learn more: [Tipping in the UK (VisitBritain)](https://www.visitbritain.com/en/food-drink/tipping-uk).

## Power & Charging
- The UK uses **Type G** plugs and **230V / 50Hz** power.  
  Learn more: [UK plug type (Electrical Safety First)](https://www.electricalsafetyfirst.org.uk/guidance/covering-all-you-need-to-know-about-plugs/).
- Bring a **Type G adapter** and a **power bank** for long walking days.

## Weather in April (Plan for Layers)
- April in the North East is **cool, breezy, and changeable**.
- Example: **Berwick-upon-Tweed** averages around **13 degC / 55 degF highs** and **4 degC / 39 degF lows** in April.  
  Learn more: [Met Office climate averages](https://www.metoffice.gov.uk/research/climate/maps-and-data/uk-climate-averages/gcuh7u0z8).

## Getting Around Once You're There
- **AD122 Hadrian's Wall Country Bus** runs seasonally and is the most useful shuttle for hikers.  
  Learn more: [AD122 overview](https://www.visithadrianswall.co.uk/explore/ad122-hadrians-wall-country-bus/).
- **Bowness-on-Solway** is served by local buses from Carlisle.  
  Learn more: [Service 93](https://www.cumberland.gov.uk/buses/service/93).
- **Rail access**: Newcastle, Hexham, Haltwhistle, and Carlisle are the main rail points.  
  Learn more: [Tyne Valley Line](https://www.scotrail.co.uk/train-times/newcastle-to-carlisle).

## Hiking Safety & Trail Conditions
- The central Wall stretch has **short, steep climbs** and can be muddy after rain.
- **Good boots, poles, and a light waterproof** are your best friends.
- The path is well-marked, but **fog** happens - download offline maps.
  Learn more: [Hadrian's Wall Path (National Trails)](https://www.nationaltrail.co.uk/en_GB/trails/hadrians-wall-path/).

## Closures & Seasonal Hours
- **English Heritage** sites can have shorter hours in early spring; check the exact opening times for each site.
  Learn more: [Housesteads](https://www.english-heritage.org.uk/visit/places/housesteads-roman-fort-hadrians-wall/) and [Birdoswald](https://www.english-heritage.org.uk/visit/places/birdoswald-roman-fort-hadrians-wall/).

## Health & Emergency
- UK emergency number: **999** (or **112** works too).
- Non-emergency medical help: **NHS 111**.
  Learn more: [NHS 111](https://www.nhs.uk/nhs-services/urgent-and-emergency-care-services/when-to-call-999/).

## Luggage Transfer & Logistics
- Baggage transfer services operate along the Wall; great if you want daypacks only.  
  Tip: book early in April, and confirm pickup times the night before.

## Mobile Data & Connectivity
- Coverage is solid in towns and patchy on ridge sections.
- Consider a **UK eSIM** for easy arrival setup.
